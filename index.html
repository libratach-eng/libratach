Why we built this

America's reading scores keep sliding. We wanted to do something about it.

The National Assessment of Educational Progress tested reading comprehension nationwide in 2024, and the results were rough. Most fourth graders are reading below the proficient level, and the share of eighth graders who can't clear even the "basic" bar is the highest it's ever been.

Libratach was built by a lifelong reader who kept seeing these numbers and wanted to build something concrete. It won't fix the whole system. It gives your kid a short daily habit that trains comprehension directly, with an AI that watches how focus holds up over a session and tells you what to try next.

Premium · AI insight

Watch focus rise and fall, then get a specific fix

Every premium session is scored against the comprehension quiz that follows it. Libratach plots how retention holds up minute by minute, then tells you exactly what to adjust.

Focus over a 20-minute session Charlotte's Web · today
✨ AI recommendation

Focus held strong for about ten minutes, then started to drop. Two adjustments usually help here:

  • Shorter sessions. Try 12-15 minutes instead of 20, then build back up.
  • Smaller chunks. Dropping from 3 words to 2 gives more time to process each phrase.

If focus drops even at a slower pace, the issue is sometimes the opposite: nudging WPM up a little can keep attention more engaged.

Works on personal texts too. Upload a school assignment or a favorite book and get the same graph, just between you and your kid.
